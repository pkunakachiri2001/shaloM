Cyber Law Legal Education Access to Justice Legal Tech

1. Problem Definition / Identification

Identified Problem: Lack of accessible, practical, and legally accurate cyber law education and guidance for citizens and law students leads to under-reporting of cyber crimes, loss of evidence, and delayed legal remedies.

Area of Law: Information Technology Act, 2000; IPC provisions (e.g., Sections 354D, 420, 506); Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023; Evidence Act (Section 65B).

Who is Affected: Students, victims of cyber fraud/harassment, and the general public who need immediate legal direction.

Limitations of Current Solutions: Official resources are scattered, text-heavy, and difficult for non-law users; there is minimal step-by-step guidance; no integrated learning + reporting flow.

Tech-Based Justification: A legal-tech app can simplify cyber law learning, guide users to identify crimes, preserve evidence, and connect to official reporting channels quickly.

2. Methodology

  1. Identify real-world cyber law problems (fraud, harassment, identity theft, privacy breaches).
  2. Review existing legal provisions (IT Act 2000, DPDP Act 2023, IPC, Evidence Act).
  3. Analyze gaps in accessibility and reporting outcomes.
  4. Conceptualize LexGuard modules: Academy, Crime Identifier, Emergency Tools, News, Career Hub.
  5. Define user flow, safeguards, and realistic app blueprint for Project 2 prototype.

3. Implementation (Conceptual Blueprint)

3.1 Functional Requirements

  • Interactive learning modules with quizzes (IT Act, cyber crimes, data protection).
  • Crime Identifier that maps user descriptions to legal categories and relevant sections.
  • Emergency toolkit: incident reporting checklist, evidence tips, complaint templates.
  • Legal news updates (cyber law, judgments, policy changes).
  • Career hub for legal-tech learning and internships.

3.2 Non-Functional Requirements

  • Privacy-first (local analysis, minimal data collection).
  • Accessibility for non-law users (simple language).
  • Mobile-first UI for quick access.
  • Offline-friendly learning modules.
  • Accuracy: legal references cited per module.
  • Ethical safeguards: clear disclaimers (not legal advice).
  • Security: no sensitive data storage by default.
  • Scalability for future prototype expansion.

3.3 Screen & User Flow (Based on App)

  • Home → Academy → Lesson → Quiz → Completion Badge
  • Home → Crime Identifier → Result → Actions → Report / Reset
  • Home → Emergency Tools → Report Form / Templates / Contacts
  • Home → News → Read Updates
  • Home → Career → Resources / Internships
Diagrams and wireframes are included in the Diagrams and Wireframes folders.

3.4 Legal Safeguards & Compliance

  • In-app disclaimer: educational only; consult legal professionals for advice.
  • Guidance to official reporting channels (cybercrime.gov.in, helpline 1930).
  • Data minimization and local-only processing for crime identification.
  • Evidence handling tips aligned with Section 65B Evidence Act.
  • Privacy rights and DPDP Act 2023 principles integrated into lessons.

4. Project Outcomes
